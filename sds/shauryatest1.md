# The Midnight Deploy

Arjun's fingers flew across the keyboard, the glow of his dual monitors cutting through the Bangalore darkness. 4:00 AM. In San Francisco, his co-founders were just finishing lunch.

"Deploy looks excellent," Sarah messaged. "Great work on the API fix."

He smiled, exhaustion forgotten. Six months ago, he'd been a college freshman juggling thermodynamics lectures and late-night coding sessions. Now he was employee #4 at a Y Combinator startup, building infrastructure that served fifty thousand users.

His mother knocked softly. "Still working?"

"Almost done, Amma."

She set down chai, fragrant with cardamom. "Don't forget—you're still eighteen."

Arjun refreshed the monitoring dashboard. Error rates dropping. Response times optimal. Somewhere in Missouri, a teacher was using their platform to plan tomorrow's lesson. In Brazil, a student was taking a practice exam.

The code he'd written at his childhood desk was touching lives across continents.

"Deployment successful," he typed into Slack.

"Crushing it as always," replied Marcus. "Get some sleep!"

Arjun sipped his chai, watching the sunrise paint his window orange. Engineering school started in four hours. He'd learned more in six months at the startup than in three semesters of lectures.

He closed his laptop, already thinking about tomorrow's features.
