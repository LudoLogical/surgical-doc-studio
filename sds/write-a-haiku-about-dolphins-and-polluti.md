# Haiku: Dolphins and Pollution

## Poem

Plastic waves surround  
Dolphins leap through murky depths—  
Ocean chokes on waste

## Context

This haiku juxtaposes the grace and vitality of dolphins against the grim reality of marine pollution. The 5-7-5 syllable structure captures a moment of tension between natural beauty and environmental degradation.

### Imagery Breakdown

- **Line 1**: "Plastic waves surround" (5 syllables) — Establishes the polluted setting
- **Line 2**: "Dolphins leap through murky depths" (7 syllables) — Depicts marine life persisting despite contamination
- **Line 3**: "Ocean chokes on waste" (5 syllables) — Delivers the stark environmental message

### Thematic Elements

The haiku employs traditional nature imagery while addressing contemporary environmental concerns, creating a contrast between:

- The dolphins' freedom of movement and the ocean's imprisonment by pollution
- Natural elegance and human-caused degradation
- Life's persistence and environmental threat
